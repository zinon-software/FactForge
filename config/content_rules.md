# Content Rules — Never Violate
# These rules apply to every video, every script, every thumbnail, every title.

---

## ALWAYS DO

- Verify every statistic from minimum 2 official sources before writing any script
- Cite all sources in every video description
- Use exact numbers with full context: "$400 billion in today's money, adjusted for inflation"
- Present historical facts as historical facts, not as modern political arguments
- Frame sensitive topics educationally: "medical economics", "defense budget analysis", "historical data"
- Keep all content within YouTube Community Guidelines
- Include a qualifier when citing estimates: "historians estimate", "according to the World Bank"
- Use the most recent available data (note the year)

---

## NEVER DO

- Publish any statistic that cannot be verified from Tier 1 or Tier 2 sources
- Make religious rulings (fatwas), theological judgments, or declare one religion superior
- Attack, mock, or demean specific living individuals (public figures may be discussed factually)
- Use copyrighted music — voice narration and natural sound effects only
- Sensationalize tragedies, disasters, or deaths for views
- Make political endorsements or tell viewers how to vote
- Publish health/medical advice as fact without WHO or peer-reviewed sources
- Create thumbnails showing content not in the video (misleading thumbnails)
- Use footage, images, or music without confirmed rights (Pexels = safe)
- Make absolute claims about contested historical events without acknowledging debate

---

## SENSITIVE TOPICS — Allowed With Strict Framing

These topics are allowed but require specific framing:

| Topic | How to Frame It |
|-------|----------------|
| Organ prices | "Medical economics and healthcare costs globally" — cite WHO and academic sources |
| Military power rankings | "Defense budget comparisons" — cite SIPRI data specifically |
| Religious historical achievements | "Historical and archaeological record" — cite historians, not theologians |
| Wealth inequality | "Economic data and distribution statistics" — cite World Bank, IMF |
| Country comparisons | "Official data from [source]" — never imply one culture is superior |
| Colonial history | Present facts neutrally — cite historians from multiple perspectives |
| Ancient empire sizes | "Historians estimate" — acknowledge measurement methodology |

---

## Fact-Check Source Trust Hierarchy

Use in this order:

1. **World Bank, IMF, UN, WHO, CIA World Factbook, SIPRI** — highest authority
2. **Forbes, Bloomberg, Reuters, AP, Statista** — strong, verify methodology
3. **Academic papers, Britannica, World History Encyclopedia** — strong for history
4. **Wikipedia** — use ONLY to find citations, then verify the citation itself
5. **News articles, blogs, YouTube** — NOT acceptable as sole source

---

## The One Rule That Overrides Everything

**When in doubt, don't publish.**

It is better to skip an idea than to publish a mistake. One wrong statistic can destroy channel credibility that took months to build. Flag uncertain facts, find a better-documented idea, and move on.
